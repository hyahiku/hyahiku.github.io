01

Overview & Motivation

Why This Analysis?

As an e-commerce business owner, understanding the voice of the customer is a strategic priority. This analysis investigates Amazon reviews for beauty products to surface structured, recurring patterns in negative customer experiences.

Two core priorities drove the approach: understanding what customers complain about with competitor products, and identifying differentiation opportunities to improve the overall customer experience.

Research Question

What are the common themes found in customer feedback with low ratings?

By exploring this, we can identify areas for product improvement, satisfy customer expectations, differentiate from competitors, and improve product rankings and trust.

A similar methodology could be applied to other aspects of e-commerce — including market research, keyword utilization, and SEO strategy — making this a broadly transferable analytical framework.

The Dataset

The Amazon Beauty Reviews dataset is sourced from Hugging Face. It contains 701,528 rows across 10 columns. The columns most relevant to this analysis are rating, title, and text.

Rating Distribution

The dataset spans ratings 1–5. Low ratings (≤ 3 stars) account for 29% of all reviews — approximately 1 in 3 customers not having an exceptional experience. From a market research perspective, this proportion is notable but not alarming, and provides a meaningful signal to analyze.

Missing Values

Only title and text contained missing values. Rows where both were empty were removed. Rows with at least one value present were retained — reflecting how Amazon allows customers to write in either or both fields.

03

Method & Analysis Techniques

The analysis was organized into three sequential phases. Each phase builds on the previous, ensuring data quality before applying NLP and statistical methods.

01
Data Quality Assessment
Comprehensive cleaning to remove values that could skew results. Focused on duplicates, missing values, and irrelevant characters. Rows with both title and text empty were dropped; rows with one present were retained.
02
NLP on Low Reviews
Filtered to ratings ≤ 3. Combined title + text into full_text. Applied stopword removal — both standard English and custom domain-specific beauty terms — to isolate meaningful signal words.
03
Statistical Clustering
K-Means applied to group reviews by similar language patterns. Produced 5 clusters, each with top associated words and 3 representative text examples. Clusters were labeled and further broken into specific complaint drivers.

Phase 1 — Data Cleaning in Detail

The cleaning process emphasized validity over convenience. Skimping on this step risks biased data that misrepresents complaint themes — particularly important when the goal is to understand the hierarchy and proportions of complaints.

Duplicate Removal

Duplicate rows were identified and removed. Leaving duplicates would artificially inflate the frequency of certain words and phrases, distorting the proportions seen in clustering.

Missing Value Strategy

Rather than dropping all rows with any missing field, only rows where both title and text were empty were removed. This preserved reviews written only in the title or only in the text body.

Character Normalization

Trailing spaces, special characters, and noise were stripped from text fields to prevent them from interfering with tokenization and word frequency analysis downstream.

Column Consolidation

title and text were merged into a single full_text column. This simplifies analysis while capturing the full customer voice — some customers write most of their feedback in the title.

Phase 2 — Stopword Removal

Stopwords are non-meaningful words (e.g. "the", "is", "a") that take up space without adding analytical value. Two layers of stopwords were applied:

Standard English Stopwords

Common functional words sourced from NLTK's English stopword list — prepositions, articles, conjunctions, and common verbs that appear across all domains.

Custom Domain Stopwords

Beauty-specific terms that are ubiquitous but uninformative in this context — e.g. "product", "use", "hair", "skin" — which would otherwise dominate word frequencies without revealing complaint themes.

Phase 3 — K-Means Clustering

K-Means was chosen as the preferred clustering method because it groups reviews by similar language patterns and produces interpretable cluster centroids. The result was 5 clusters, each visualized with its top associated words and 3 representative text examples.

Key Insight

Negative experiences were not random or vague — they were structured and recurring. Quality, color, and performance issues alone account for over 64% of all complaint drivers, suggesting clear, addressable areas for product development and listing optimization.

Product Development

Quality (26.4%) and Performance (17.8%) complaints point directly to manufacturing or formulation issues. These are the highest-leverage areas for product improvement — fixing them addresses nearly half of all negative feedback.

Listing Optimization

Color (19.5%), Size (11.5%), and Quantity (8.4%) complaints often reflect a mismatch between customer expectations and product listings. Better images, clearer descriptions, and accurate measurements could reduce these significantly.

05

Conclusion

What the Data Tells Us

By applying NLP preprocessing and K-Means clustering to 701,528 Amazon beauty reviews, we were able to identify and quantify the primary drivers of customer dissatisfaction. Negative experiences were not random — they were structured, recurring, and categorizable.

From highest to lowest: quality, color, performance, size, smell, and quantity are the most prominent complaint drivers. This ranking provides a prioritized roadmap for improvement.

Business Takeaways

🔧

Product Development

Quality and performance complaints (44.2% combined) are the highest-priority improvement areas. Addressing these would have the greatest impact on customer satisfaction and ratings.

📋

Listing Accuracy

Color, size, and quantity complaints (39.4% combined) often stem from inaccurate listings. Clearer product descriptions and images could reduce these expectations gaps significantly.

🏆

Competitive Differentiation

Understanding competitor weaknesses at this level of specificity creates direct opportunities to position a product as superior in the areas customers complain about most.

🔄

Transferable Framework

This NLP + clustering pipeline can be applied to any review dataset — enabling similar insight extraction for market research, SEO keyword analysis, or customer experience audits.
